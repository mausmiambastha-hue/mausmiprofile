✉️ A Letter from Mausmi
Curiosity has quietly shaped most of the choices in my life.

Hello,

If you're reading this, you're probably wondering who I am.

I'm Mausmi — a 2x founder, fashion technologist, teacher, and a curious builder who enjoys understanding how complex systems work and then trying to improve them.

Let me take you quickly through the journey of my life.

I grew up in Patna, where the career paths people talked about were simple: engineer, doctor, or IAS officer. Building companies wasn't really part of the script.

But I was always curious about how things worked — especially the systems behind everyday things. Factories fascinated me. So did supply chains. Over time that curiosity pulled me toward an unusual intersection: technology, fashion, and manufacturing.

Years ago I co-founded ThreadSol, where we built algorithms that help garment factories decide how thousands of pattern pieces should be cut from massive rolls of fabric. Fabric is the most expensive input in making a garment, so even small improvements matter.

Over time the software spread across factories around the world. At one point we realized those factories were producing nearly 1% of the world's garments — a thought that still surprises me.

ThreadSol was eventually acquired by Coats PLC (UK) for $12M.

After that, I did what founders tend to do — I started building again. Today I'm working on ZapScale, an AI-first Customer Success platform for SaaS companies.

Along the way I've also been a teacher, writer, and storyteller — writing books and articles on manufacturing and entrepreneurship, and occasionally joining podcasts to talk about building companies and systems.

These days I'm also building Shifu Foundation, an idea that excites me deeply. The vision is simple but ambitious: to inspire and enable one technology founder from every district of India.

I grew up outside the startup hubs, so this feels personal. Talent exists everywhere — sometimes it just needs a spark.

Outside of work my interests range widely — technology, sustainability, fashion, AI, manufacturing, startups, founders, art, and travel.

But at the end of the day, the things that matter most to me are simple: family, health, curiosity, and happiness.

Warmly,
Mausmi

Still curious. Still building.
